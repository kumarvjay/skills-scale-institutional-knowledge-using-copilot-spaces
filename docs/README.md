# OctoAcme Project Management Docs — README

OctoAcme Project Management Docs centralize proven processes used by our team to deliver, release, and continually improve projects. This README summarizes our methodology and offers direct links to each doc for quick navigation.

## Project Management Processes Summary

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. The organization applies a five-phase framework across all cross-functional projects: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Close & Retrospective**.

### Key Characteristics

- **Clear Ownership**: Each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead who coordinate delivery, define outcomes, and measure success.
- **Lifecycle Approach**: Every project progresses through distinct phases with specific deliverables and decision gates to validate readiness before moving forward.
- **Iterative Delivery**: Work is broken into shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria, allowing teams to deliver value incrementally and gather feedback.
- **Data-Informed Decisions**: Success is measured against defined metrics, and retrospectives drive continuous improvement based on evidence.
- **Quality Built-In**: Testing, security scanning, and code review are integral to execution, not afterthoughts.
- **Risk-Aware**: Teams actively identify, assess, and mitigate risks throughout the lifecycle, with clear escalation paths and regular status updates.
- **Psychological Safety**: The culture encourages feedback, learning, and blameless retrospectives.

### Typical Workflow

1. **Initiation**: Validate business need, define success metrics, identify stakeholders, and create a lightweight Project One-pager as a decision gate.
2. **Planning**: Break work into prioritized backlog items with detailed acceptance criteria, estimate scope, define Definition of Done, and map dependencies.
3. **Execution**: Daily standups, weekly syncs, pull request reviews, continuous testing, and risk monitoring to maintain momentum and visibility.
4. **Release**: Deploy to production following a pre-release checklist, run smoke tests, announce to stakeholders, and prepare rollback plans.
5. **Retrospective & Improvement**: Capture learnings, convert them into action items with clear owners, and integrate improvements back into processes and the product.

### Communication Strategy

- **Daily Standups** (15 min): Focus on progress, blockers, and dependencies.
- **Weekly Delivery Sync**: Show progress, updates, and flagged risks to the leadership and stakeholders.
- **Weekly PM/PdM Alignment**: Ensure product decisions and delivery are coordinated.
- **Monthly Stakeholder Updates**: Keep sponsors informed of milestone progress and any material changes.
- **Sprint/Milestone Demos**: Showcase completed work and gather feedback.
- **Retrospectives** (45–75 min): Timebox reflections after sprints, releases, or incidents to drive continuous improvement.

### Core Roles

- **Project Manager**: Coordinates delivery, manages schedules, risks, and communications.
- **Product Manager**: Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures success.
- **Developers**: Implement features, collaborate on design, and contribute to planning and testing.
- **QA/Testing**: Validate quality and acceptance criteria.
- **Stakeholders**: Provide inputs, approvals, and strategic direction.
